Terminal Radio — Free Internet Radio for Coding & Focus

Terminal Radio is a curated internet radio experience built for developers, designers, and anyone who needs a focused sonic backdrop while they work. Styled with the same terminal aesthetic as the rest of TerminalFeed, it brings together some of the best ambient, lo-fi, and downtempo streams from across the internet — all in one dark, distraction-free interface.

The streams featured on Terminal Radio are provided by SomaFM, one of the internet's longest-running independent radio stations. Founded in 2000 by Rusty Hodge in San Francisco, SomaFM is entirely listener-supported and completely ad-free. Every stream is hand-curated by real DJs who care deeply about the music they play. SomaFM has been a staple of the internet radio landscape for over two decades, and we are proud to feature their streams here.

Research has consistently shown that ambient and lo-fi music can improve concentration, reduce stress, and help maintain a flow state during deep work. Unlike music with prominent vocals or unpredictable changes, ambient and downtempo genres provide a consistent sonic texture that supports focus without demanding attention. This makes them ideal companions for coding sessions, writing, studying, or any task that requires sustained concentration.

Our station selection covers a range of moods and styles. Groove Salad delivers smooth ambient and downtempo beats — it has been one of SomaFM's most popular channels since its launch. Drone Zone drifts into deeper, more atmospheric territory with textured ambient soundscapes. DEF CON Radio brings dark ambient, industrial, and synthwave with a hacker edge — named after the famous security conference. Space Station Soma offers spaced-out mid-tempo electronica, while Lush features sensuous, mellow vocals. Vaporwaves serves up the nostalgic, slowed-down aesthetic of the vaporwave movement. n5MD Radio showcases ambient and post-rock from the respected n5MD label, and cliqhop idm delivers intelligent dance music with intricate beats and clicks.

Terminal Radio also features a rotating spotlight on independent artists whose music fits the coding and focus aesthetic. Our featured artist program highlights musicians who create atmospheric, cinematic, and ambient works perfect for late-night sessions. These artists are often independent creators pushing boundaries in electronic and ambient music.

Everything here is free to use. There are no ads in the audio streams — that is the SomaFM promise. The interface is designed to stay out of your way: pick a station, hit play, and get back to work. Your last selected station and volume are remembered automatically so you can jump right back in. Terminal Radio is just one part of the TerminalFeed ecosystem of tools, feeds, and utilities built for people who live in the terminal.
