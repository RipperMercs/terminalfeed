Week 1: Bitcoin Fear is Real, AI Agents Are Coming, and Nobody's Paying Attention

By Ripper March 16, 2026 5 min read

Welcome to the first weekly dispatch from TerminalFeed. Every week I sit in front of the dashboard, watch the feeds, and write down what stands out. This is not a newsletter with polished takes and affiliate links. It is raw signal from someone who stares at real-time data for a living. Let's get into it.

Bitcoin at $71K with Extreme Fear

Bitcoin is hovering around $71,000 and the Fear & Greed Index is sitting at 15. That is deep in Extreme Fear territory. If you are new here, the scale goes from 0 (maximum panic) to 100 (maximum euphoria), and anything below 25 qualifies as Extreme Fear. So the market is terrified right now.

But here is the thing that makes this interesting: the price is not crashing. It is holding. BTC is at $71K, which would have been a moonshot number two years ago, and yet sentiment is acting like the sky is falling. That disconnect is worth paying attention to.

When price is stable or rising and fear is this elevated, it usually means one of two things. Either there is a macro risk that everyone is pricing in but has not materialized yet, or the market simply has not processed the bullish case. Both scenarios tend to resolve with a sharp move. The question is which direction.

Historically, readings below 20 on the Fear & Greed Index have been strong accumulation zones for long-term holders. That does not mean today is the bottom. It means the crowd is scared, and scared crowds have a track record of being wrong at the extremes. If you are watching this on the TerminalFeed dashboard, keep an eye on whether this number stays pinned below 20 or starts climbing. The shift in direction matters more than the absolute number.

AI Agents Are the Internet's Newest Users

Something I noticed while building TerminalFeed that I think most people are not paying enough attention to: AI agents are becoming real internet users. Not in a theoretical "someday AI will browse the web" sense. Right now. Today.

We opened a public API a few weeks ago, and within days AI models started calling our /api/briefing endpoint to pull real-time world data. Claude, GPT, Perplexity, and others. They need current information because their training data has a cutoff, and they are actively seeking sources that give them structured, up-to-date feeds.

Think about what that means. These models are not just answering questions from a static knowledge base anymore. They are browsing, calling APIs, pulling live data, and citing sources. They behave like researchers with internet access, because that is exactly what they are becoming.

The sites that make their data AI-accessible now will have a massive advantage as this trend accelerates. If your API returns clean JSON with real-time data, you become a source that AI agents rely on. If your site is a JavaScript-heavy mess with no structured data, you are invisible to the next generation of internet users. This is not a future prediction. It is already happening on our API right now.

Quick stat: AI agent traffic to the TerminalFeed API has been growing steadily since launch. These are not bots scraping pages. They are language models making intentional API calls to get structured data for their users.

What I'm Watching This Week

The Fed meeting is approaching, and prediction markets currently have a June rate cut priced at 62% probability. That number is the one to watch. If it moves above 70% this week, expect risk assets (crypto included) to rally on the sentiment shift alone. The actual decision matters less than the market's belief about the decision, and prediction markets are the best real-time gauge we have for that belief.

Meanwhile, GitHub Trending has been dominated by AI coding tools. Cursor, Claude Code, Windsurf. The developer tools landscape is shifting faster than at any point since the cloud computing revolution. Every week, a new tool appears that makes the previous week's workflow feel outdated. If you are a developer and you are not watching these trends in real-time, you are already behind. The window to adopt and adapt is measured in weeks now, not years.

On the macro side, I am keeping an eye on Treasury yields and the dollar index. Both have been quietly drifting in a direction that historically favors risk-on assets, but nobody is talking about it because the Fear & Greed number is stealing all the attention. The boring charts are often the ones that matter most.

One more thing: if you are building anything that touches real-time data, now is the time to think about how AI agents will consume your product. Not next quarter. Now. The tools are here, the agents are active, and the first-mover advantage in this space is very real.

See you next week. The terminal never sleeps.
